Justin Timberlake shares that he penned '100 songs' for his upcoming album, 'Everything I Thought It Was.

In the quest for perfection with his freshly announced album, "Everything I Thought It Was," Justin Timberlake faced the daunting task
of separating the cream of the musical crop from his overflowing stash of tunes. In a recent chat with Zane Lowe on Apple Music 1,
the 42-year-old pop sensation spilled the beans on the album-making chaos. Timberlake, who clearly believes in quality over quantity,
confessed to making some tough choices, cutting down from a whopping 100 songs to a mere 18 for his eagerly awaited record, set to drop
on March 15. With a laugh, he added, "I worked on this album for what feels like a century, and now I have the challenge of convincing you
that these 18 songs are better than the other 82 discarded masterpieces. It's like picking your favorite child, but with more bass lines and
fewer bedtime stories." Get ready for Timberlake's musical parenting skills to be put to the ultimate test!



Vampire Weekend to Embark on 2024 Tour, Releases Two New Singles

Vampire Weekend has announced a 2024 tour in support of their forthcoming album “Only God Above Us,” in addition to releasing the album’s first two singles “Capricorn” and “Gen-X Cops.” After the album’s arrival on April 5, the North American jaunt will kick off on Apr. 8 in Austin, TX, and make stops in more than 35 cities such as Los Angeles, Berkeley, Seattle, Cincinnati, Toronto, Boston, New York and Atlanta. The band will then return to Austin for one final show on Oct. 17. Pre-sale tickets will become exclusively available to Citi card members on Tuesday, Feb. 20 at 10 a.m. local time through Feb. 22 at 10 p.m. General on sale will begin Friday, Feb. 23 at 10 a.m. local time.
